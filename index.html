About Me

Ever since I can remember, I have enjoyed finding and manipulating patterns. That satisfaction has led me to a lifelong love of chess where I am constantly looking for ways to create, bend, and cajole the patterns of the pieces on the chess board to my advantage. It also contributed to my study of mathematics and science with a career as an analytical chemist.

During the pandemic, my company reduced their work force and I found myself at a crossroads. Uncertain about searching for work during the pandemic, I discovered the opportunity to enroll in the Eleven Fifty Academy Software Development Bootcamp. The immersive coding experience of Eleven Fifty Acadamy guided my journey toward learning and improving my command of C#, ASP.NET, MVC, APIs, HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap.

Like chess, software development is very much about identifying, using, and manipulating patterns in order to solve problems. I have a lot of experience with that both in my chemistry career and in studying chess puzzles and material. I look forward to applying that experience to the field of software development as I continue on the next part of my journey.
